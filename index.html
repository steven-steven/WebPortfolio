About Me


Often times, the solution may seem obvious. But then humanity don't stop there. We keep finding a better solution and refining our problems. The reason I decided to study Computer Engineering is not to simply know solutions, but to seek new and better solutions than what had already been made in the past
I am a persistent thinker that love challanges, is focused, and capable of innovating and learning quickly in any role I take. Self-motivated to learn how technologies works to create the things we enjoy today and all other possible things we can enjoy in the future.

Background

I was born in 1999, and grew up in my country - Indonesia. On September 2016, I decided to come to Canada to pursue a 5-year Computer Engineering degree in University of Waterloo. What brought me here was the co-op and entrepreuneurship opportunity University of Waterloo is known for, because I believe this sort of environment will help me succeed, learn, and continue to open-mindedly grow.
I love a lot of things. From the tangible arduino kits to programs that make up games and applications. When I am able to do these things, I feel I am one step ahead to understanding the world I am living in today.
Goals

Short-Term: Aside from everything technical, I am currently focusing to improve one thing I have often had difficulty with - Communication. Now that I am away from home, I will try to meet all sorts of people everyday, join events, and staying out of my comfort zone. Only recently had I begin reading books again, and I am nontheless enjoying it.
Long-Term: Build incredible things. Learning new programming skills, working with people of the same interest, joining hackathons to build new awesome wierd inventions, had always been my goal. Now that I'm already there, I feel like all I had to do is continue doing what I have always enjoyed, always giving my best at everything, and see where it will bring me next.
